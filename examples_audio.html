Audio Examples

The SNES has a dedicated audio processor – the SPC700 – with its own 64KB of RAM, its own DSP chip, and its own instruction set. It runs independently from the main CPU. The two communicate through just 4 shared I/O bytes.

These examples use SNESMOD, a tracker-based audio engine. You write .it files in a tracker (OpenMPT, Schism Tracker, etc.), the smconv tool converts them to SPC700 format, and the library handles playback from C.

Examples

Example Difficulty Description
snesmod_music Intermediate Tracker music playback with smconv and the SNESMOD process loop
snesmod_sfx Intermediate Playing sound effects alongside music

Prerequisites

  • Build the smconv tool: make tools
  • A tracker module in .it format (Impulse Tracker)

Key Concepts

  • The SPC700 runs its own program independently of the 65816 CPU
  • SNESMOD uploads its driver + song data to SPC700 RAM at init
  • Call spcProcess() every frame to keep audio synchronized
  • Sound effects use separate channels and can play over music

Start with snesmod_music to get music playing, then move to snesmod_sfx to layer sound effects on top.
